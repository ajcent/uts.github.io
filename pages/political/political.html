Political Self

Originating from the Greek word Politiká, this involves making decisions that affect communities, states, or nations. It is the practice of governing and controlling a group of people. A crucial aspect of one's political self is the ability to influence others through decision-making.

Factors

People can be influenced through experience

  • Normative Influence

    This social influence stems from conforming to positive expectations. People tend to avoid behaviors that might result in social punishment or disapproval.

  • Informational Influence

    This type of influence is based on accepting information obtained from others as evidence of reality.

Challenges

Problems that college students experience

Ineffective leadership

College students may face challenges when they encounter leaders who are ineffective in motivating and guiding the group. This can hinder their ability to achieve goals and may lead to frustration or a sense of disengagement.

Absence of leadership

When leaders exhibit laissez-faire leadership, where they are absent, hesitant to make decisions, or fail to take action when needed, it can lead to a lack of direction and structure within the group. This can create confusion, inefficiency, and hinder progress.

Mismatched styles

Different leadership styles may be present in various contexts, such as classrooms, student organizations, or extracurricular activities. If the leadership style employed by a leader does not align with the expectations and needs of the followers, it can lead to a lack of cohesion, ineffective communication, and reduced motivation among students.

Limited development

College students may face challenges if they do not have sufficient opportunities to develop their own leadership skills and qualities. This can hinder their ability to effectively lead and influence others, impacting their personal and professional growth.

Autocratic leadership

If college students are exposed to autocratic leaders who are excessively directive and non-participative, it can restrict their creativity, autonomy, and participation in decision-making processes. This can result in a lack of personal growth and diminished engagement.

Understanding

A new perspective

Leadership and politics significantly contribute to self-understanding. They allow individuals to explore values, beliefs, and principles, enhancing self-identity. These experiences foster self-confidence and the development of essential skills. They also promote self-awareness by exposing individuals to diverse perspectives and challenges, encouraging introspection and understanding personal strengths and limitations. Furthermore, effective communication and influence skills gained in leadership and politics extend beyond these realms, benefiting relationships and self-expression. Overall, leadership and political engagement offer opportunities for personal growth, self-reflection, and the formation of a well-rounded sense of self.
