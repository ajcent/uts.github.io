Material Self

The concept of the material self in psychology encompasses the things we own, the relationships we value, and the objects we consider as integral parts of our identity. Alongside the social self and the spiritual self, it forms an essential component of our overall sense of self.

Factors

People can be influenced through experience

  • Body

    The body is the most intimate aspect of the material self. It is the physical vessel that we inhabit and it is often seen as a reflection of our personality and identity.

  • Clothes

    Clothes are another important aspect of the material self. They can be used to express our personal style, to project a certain image, or to simply feel comfortable and confident.

  • Immediate family

    Immediate family members, such as our parents, siblings, and children, are also considered part of the material self. They are the people who are closest to us and who we share our lives with.

  • Home

    The home is often seen as a reflection of our personality and values. It is a place where we feel safe, comfortable, and secure.

  • Physical

    The material self is often described in physical terms, such as the body, clothes, and home.

  • Social

    The material self is also social in nature, as it is influenced by our relationships with family, friends, and colleagues.

  • Symbolic

    The material self can also be symbolic, as possessions can be used to represent our status, values, or personality.

  • Emotional

    The material self can also be emotional, as our possessions can evoke feelings of comfort, security, or pride.

Challenges

Problems that college students experience

Pressure to conform

Some students may experience pressure to own particular material possessions or engage in particular activities in order to fit in or satisfy social expectations as a result of college culture and societal norms. This stress may be financially exhausting, lead to feelings of inadequacy, and may have a negative impact on the individuals' mental health.

Financial Uncertainty

Many college students have financial issues that make it challenging for them to acquire the supplies they need. They can have trouble affording the materials, equipment, textbooks, and other things they need for their study.The cost of attending college is expensive, which is quite difficult for both students and their families. The costs of going to college, including housing, food plans, and transportation, can be stressful financially and have an effect on a student's material well-being.

Balancing work and study

Some college students work part-time or full-time jobs while they are enrolled in classes to ease their financial worries. This effort might be difficult to handle since it can be overwhelming for them. As students handle the necessity to pay bills with the desire to maintain their academic achievement, finding a balance between work and academics becomes challenging.

Accessibility Challenges

Access to material resources differs among college students. Certain children originate from underprivileged or disadvantaged backgrounds, making it harder for them to get the supplies they need than those who are more fortunate.In the modern digital era, technology is incredibly important to education. If a college student does not have access to a dependable computer, an internet connection, or the software needed for courses, it may be difficult for them to fully engage in online learning or finish assignments.

Understanding

A new perspective

Material self refers to the physical components of our sense of self. It plays an important role in our entire notion of self, defining our identity and impacting our self-concept, interpersonal interactions, and connection to the outside world. It provides a concrete framework through which we experience ourselves in relation to others, contributing to our feeling of uniqueness and individuality.

The material self also provides a means for self-expression. The clothes we wear, the accessories we choose, and the way we adorn ourselves with personal belongings allow us to communicate aspects of our identity and personality to others. Material self can benefit an individual and improve their self-esteem, self-image, and confidence.
